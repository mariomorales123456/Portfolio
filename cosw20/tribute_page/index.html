Winston Churchill

1874-1965

WW1

Churchill's career began in 1900. He was elected as a member of parliament in 1904, and began his climb up the ranks for about a decade. He became Lord Admiral (head of the Royal Navy) at that time. WW1 he found himself in trouble since at that time a campaign in Galllipoli had disastrous results, undermining his leadership and resulting in his resignation. He elected to the fight the rest of the war on the western front in france. Post-war he lost his election for parliament and remained out of office until the outbreak of WW2.

WW2

Churchill remained politically active while he was out of office - writing and giving speeches. He strongly opposed german appeasement, and when the then current prime minister Chamberlain resigned due to poor performance Churchill was elected the new prime minister. Churchill's leadership during the war is regarded as crucial in achieving victory over the nazis. His personality and speeches kept british morale high even when germany had destroyed many british cities through bombing. He also led successful campaigns in South Africa and Italy - as well as coordinating with for the landing in Normandy. In 1945 after the war had ended he was unfortunately voted out but he continued to give speeches against Soviet control in eastern europe.

His Later Years

Churchill was reelected in 1951. Unforunately he had become more ill by the time he was reelected and conducted most of his duties while bedridden. He still had his charismatic speech but his leadership during his term did not result in significant changes for britain. His health continued to detiorate while in office, up until he had to resign in 1955. He passed away in 1965 and was given a state funeral.
